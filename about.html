Hi, I'm Melissa Brattoni, an actor trained at The Australian Film and Television Academy (TAFTA).
Recently, I played a supporting role, as Sophie, in the short film 'Endless Road'. Also, I have continued my involvement in the webseries 'Starting From... Now' which has received several nominations for Webfest Montr√©al. Last year, I completed the National Actor's Intensive (NAI) in Sydney.

In 2013 I worked on my first feature film 'All About E' (Dir. Louise Wadley). Throughout my career, I have attended various workshops with casting directors including Grant Thompson and Greg Apps, as well as a masterclass with West Side Actor's Studio under the guidance of Doris Younane.

I have worked as an extra on Television show sets including 'Deadly Women', 'Wonderland', 'Packed to the Rafters', Commercials (including Woolworths and Greenpeace), Web series, and a few Student films.

S In 2012, I participated in the 48 Hour Film Festival with the entry 'LunaTic' (Dir. Jeremy Kelly-Bakker) which won awards for Best Director and Best Graphics. In the same year I completed two Screen Technique Courses at the National Institute of Dramatic Art (NIDA), tutored by Les Chantery, Emily Rose Brennan and Brendon McDonall.

My passion for screen acting began when I was very young and was further ignited after an 8-week course at the Campbelltown Arts Centre.
